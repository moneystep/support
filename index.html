Privacy Policy

How We Handle Your Data

1. Data We Collect

When you create an account and use the Platform, we collect the following information:

  • Account information: username, email address, phone number, and profile photo
  • Financial information: transaction history, entry fees paid, winnings received, and wallet balance
  • Step count data: daily step totals sourced from Apple HealthKit or Google Health Connect
  • Location data: your general location at the time of account creation for eligibility verification purposes only; this data is not stored beyond the verification check
  • Device information: device token for push notifications, operating system, and app version
  • Usage data: challenge participation history, competition results, and in-app activity
  • Payment and withdrawal information: when you request a payout, the full legal name and Zelle contact (phone number or email) you provide so we can send your winnings
  • Fraud-prevention data: records relating to suspicious, irregular, or anomalous activity, including flagged step totals and related details, retained to protect the integrity of competitions

2. Health & Step Data

The Platform accesses your step count data from Apple HealthKit (iOS) or Google Health Connect (Android) for the sole purpose of tracking your performance in challenges.

Step data retention: We store your detailed daily step data for up to 90 days to support challenges of varying lengths. Daily step data older than 90 days is automatically deleted from our servers on a rolling basis. We access historical step data from your health app to calculate your rank tier but do not store the underlying records beyond this window. Aggregated or derived figures — such as your rolling step average, cumulative challenge step totals, and your final step count in a completed challenge — are kept as part of your profile and competition records and may be retained longer.

We do not use your health data for advertising, sell it to third parties, or share it with any entity outside of the Platform for any purpose other than operating the app.

You may revoke HealthKit or Health Connect permissions at any time through your device settings. Revoking access will prevent step tracking from functioning and may affect your ability to participate in challenges.

3. Third-Party Services

The Platform uses the following third-party services to operate:

  • Google Firebase — backend infrastructure, database, authentication, and push notifications. Data is stored on Firebase servers subject to Google's privacy policies.
  • Apple HealthKit / Google Health Connect — read-only access to step count data on your device.

We do not integrate advertising networks, analytics SDKs that track behavior across apps, or any other third-party data brokers.

4. Data Sharing & Sale

The Platform does not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to any third party under any circumstances.

We may disclose information only in the following limited circumstances, including but not limited to:

  • When required by law, court order, or valid legal process
  • To comply with tax reporting obligations, including IRS requirements for issuing Form 1099
  • To prevent fraud, protect the safety of users, or enforce these terms
  • In connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all assets of the Platform, in which case users will be notified

Information visible to other users: Moneystep is a social, competitive platform. To operate leaderboards, public profiles, and friend features, certain information is visible to other users — including your username, profile photo, rank tier, step averages, finishing places, career statistics (such as total winnings, entry fees paid, and net earnings), and the U.S. state associated with your account. Do not put anything in your username or profile photo that you would not want other users to see.

5. Data Security

We use industry-standard security practices including encrypted data transmission (TLS) and Firebase security rules restricting data access to authenticated users. No method of electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security but are committed to protecting your information.

6. Data Retention & Deletion

You may request deletion of your account and associated data at any time through the app's account settings or by contacting us at hello@moneystep.app. To delete your account, you must first have no active challenge, no pending challenge settlement, and a zero wallet balance — withdraw any remaining funds before requesting deletion. We will process eligible deletion requests within 30 days.

Certain records are retained even after account deletion, to the extent permitted or required by law and for legitimate business, tax, and fraud-prevention purposes. These may include financial and transaction records, payout and withdrawal records (including the name and payment contact you provided), tax-related earnings records, completed challenge results, and records of suspicious or anomalous activity. We retain these only as long as reasonably necessary for those purposes.

7. Children's Privacy

The Platform is strictly for users aged 18 and older. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 18. If we become aware that a minor has created an account, we will terminate the account and delete all associated data immediately.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify users of material changes via push notification or email. Continued use of the app after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the updated policy. The effective date at the top of this page will reflect the most recent revision.

6. Exiting a Challenge

You may voluntarily exit a challenge at any time before or after it begins. The following rules apply:

Exits are recorded in your career statistics. Exiting mid-challenge counts as a last-place finish for statistical purposes.

7. Step Data Sync Requirements

To ensure accurate leaderboard standings and final results, users are responsible for the following:

  • A connected step source is required. The Platform reads your step data from Apple Health (iOS) or Google Health Connect (Android). You are responsible for ensuring a step source is actively recording steps into your health app and that Moneystep has been granted permission to read it. On Android in particular, Health Connect does not record steps on its own — it only stores step data provided by another app, such as your phone's health app or a connected wearable. If no step source is feeding your health app, or if permission is not granted, your steps will register as zero regardless of your physical activity. It is your sole responsibility to confirm your steps are being recorded before and during a challenge.
  • You must choose a single step source, and only that source counts. Before enrolling, you are required to select one step source for the Platform to count from. Only steps recorded by the source you select will be counted toward your challenge results — steps from any other app, device, or source will not be included. The source you select is locked to your entry at the time you enroll and cannot be changed for the duration of that challenge. It is your responsibility to choose the source that accurately reflects the steps you physically take and to keep that source recording throughout the challenge. If your selected source stops recording or syncing, your step total may stop advancing, and the most recently synced total will be used. Manually entered steps are never counted, regardless of source.
  • Keep your apps synced during a challenge. During an active challenge, make sure your steps and any connected health or fitness apps (such as your wearable's companion app, for example Garmin Connect) are synced and up to date, and open Moneystep at least once per calendar day. The Platform reads your step data from Apple Health or Google Health Connect when you open the app, and those services only contain your latest steps if your phone and any connected apps have synced.
  • Post-challenge sync window. After a challenge has ended, make sure your steps and any connected health or fitness apps are synced and up to date, and open Moneystep within 24 hours of the challenge end time, so your complete step data is captured before results are finalized.
  • Settlement timing. Challenge results are finalized approximately 24 hours after the challenge end time. Step data not synced by settlement will not be included in your final result.
  • Incomplete sync liability. If you fail to sync within the 24-hour post-challenge window, your recorded step count at the time of settlement will be used as your final total. The Platform is not responsible for any difference between your actual steps and your synced step count.
  • Syncing depends on your apps and on opening Moneystep. The Platform syncs your steps when you open Moneystep, and it can only read steps that your phone and connected apps have already synced into Apple Health or Google Health Connect. The Platform does not perform reliable automatic syncing while the app is closed or in the background, and you should not rely on background syncing for any reason. Keeping your health and fitness apps synced and up to date, and opening Moneystep to sync — including within the post-challenge window described above — is your responsibility.
  • Android battery optimization. Some Android device manufacturers (including but not limited to Samsung, Xiaomi, Huawei, and OnePlus) apply aggressive battery saving features that may prevent Moneystep from receiving background notifications. If you use one of these devices, we recommend exempting Moneystep from battery optimization in your device settings. You can typically find this setting under Battery → App optimization or Battery → Background usage limits. The Platform is not responsible for missed notifications or incomplete step data resulting from manufacturer battery optimization settings.
  • No liability for device or connectivity issues. The Platform is not responsible for incomplete, missing, or delayed step data resulting from device settings, device failures, manufacturer restrictions, network outages, or failure to sync within required timeframes.
